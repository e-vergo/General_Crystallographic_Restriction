2 The Psi Function and Main Definitions

The main theorem requires the definition of the \(\psi \) function, which measures the “arithmetic complexity” of a positive integer \(m\). For a prime power \(p^k\), we define:

  • \(\psi _{pp}(p,k) = \varphi (p^k)\) for \(p\) odd or \(k \geq 2\)

  • \(\psi _{pp}(2,1) = 0\)

For a general integer \(m\) with prime factorization \(m = \prod _i p_i^{k_i}\), we have:

\[ \psi (m) = \sum _i \psi _{pp}(p_i, k_i) \]
